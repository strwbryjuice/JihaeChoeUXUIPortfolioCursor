Project Overview
Summary
Background
36% of UK young adults face financial fragility due to a "Financial Habit Gap" formed during their teenage years, where classroom/parental instruction fails to translate into practical financial actions.
Goal
To close this gap by transforming passive financial knowledge into proactive, daily routines during the critical 14–16 young age.
Solution
A Financial Navigator that systemizes autonomy through flexible budgeting and frictionless parental guidance, secured by a human-in-the-loop AI mentorship model.

"14–16 UK teenagers lack a proactive system to manage irregular income and build financial habits, while parents struggle to provide practical financial advice, resulting in a cycle of financial anxiety and 'shallow' education."

How Might We

"How might we design a proactive financial system for 14–16 UK teens that transforms 'shallow' education into 'confident' habits by balancing teen autonomy with frictionless parental guidance?"

Usability Testings
First Round · Wireframe Stage
Wireframe stage testing with teens to evaluate core functions.
AimTo explore ideas for additional features and test whether the current functions were understandable and usable for teens.
MethodThink aloud method for 30–45 minutes per session
Who3 teenagers directly from the interview pool
Finding 01
Result3/3 participants did not realize that onboarding had finished and that they had arrived at the homepage.
InsightTeens needed a clearer visual signal to understand when one stage ended and another began. The transition from onboarding to the homepage was not explicit enough.
ChangeAdded a congratulatory pictogram at the end of onboarding to clearly signal completion and visually show the transition to the homepage.

Takeaway
Meaningful financial education is not about memorizing vocabulary. It is a habit.

By turning awkward economic lectures from school and household into more natural conversations, financial stability slowly gets formed among teens before becoming an adult.
